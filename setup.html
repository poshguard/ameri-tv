AMERI AUTO GROUP — Showroom TV

Put our live inventory on the TV

The TV shows every car currently on ameriautogroup.com — photo, price, mileage — and updates itself. Once it's set up, the only thing anyone ever does is turn the TV on.

tinyurl.com/ameri-tv
Type this into the TV's web browser. (Full address: poshguard.github.io/ameri-tv)

One-time setup — Fire TV Stick (recommended, ~$30)

  1. Plug the Fire TV Stick into the TV's HDMI port, and plug its power cable into a wall outlet using the included adapter — not the TV's USB port (that causes freezes and random shut-offs).
  2. Connect it to the store's main password-protected Wi-Fi — not a guest network that shows a sign-in page (the stick keeps dropping off those).
  3. The first time, the stick asks you to sign in with an Amazon account — ask Ulugbek to do this part (takes 5 minutes, only once).
  4. From the stick's app store, search for and install Silk Browser (free, by Amazon).
  5. Open Silk and type tinyurl.com/ameri-tv in the address bar. The display appears.
  6. Steer the round cursor anywhere over the cars with the remote's ring and press the center Select button once — it goes fullscreen. (Pressing again takes it back out, so press once and put the remote down.)
  7. Save it: open the Silk menu and choose Add to bookmarks. Next time it's one click.
  8. Stop the screensaver: on the Fire TV home screen go to Settings → Display & Sounds → Screensaver → Start time → Never.
  9. Test it: leave the TV alone for 30 minutes. If the screen goes dark or jumps back to the Fire TV home screen, tell Ulugbek — the stick needs a one-time technical tweak to disable its sleep timer. Don't try to fix it yourself.

Or: the TV's built-in browser (Samsung, LG, etc.)

  1. Open the TV's web browser app (usually called "Internet" on Samsung, "Web Browser" on LG).
  2. Type tinyurl.com/ameri-tv and press OK.
  3. Click once for fullscreen, and bookmark the page.

If you can't find a browser app after a minute of looking, the TV doesn't have one (Roku and Vizio TVs usually don't) — use the Fire TV Stick option above. Built-in TV browsers also tend to be slower and occasionally exit on their own, which is why the Stick is the recommended option.

TV settings — so it stays on all day

  • Turn off the sleep timer and "auto power off".
  • Turn off eco / energy-saving modes that dim or switch off the screen.
  • Turn off the TV's own screensaver.
Daily routine: turn the TV on and open the bookmark. That's everything. Cars, prices, and photos sync from the website automatically every few hours. If you see cable TV or a blank screen instead, press the Input/Source button on the TV remote and pick the HDMI the Fire TV Stick is plugged into.

Common questions

We sold a car — how do I take it off the TV?
You don't. When it's removed from ameriautogroup.com, it drops off the TV by itself within a few hours.
It says "Loading inventory…" and never finishes.
The TV lost its internet connection. Reconnect the Wi-Fi — the screen fixes itself within a minute.
The screen is frozen or blank.
Unplug the TV (or the Fire TV Stick) for 10 seconds, plug it back in, and open the bookmark again. If it still looks wrong, check the TV is on the right Input/Source (HDMI) — after a power cycle some TVs come back on a different input, which looks broken but isn't.
A price looks wrong.
The TV shows exactly what the website shows. Fix the price on ameriautogroup.com and the TV follows.
